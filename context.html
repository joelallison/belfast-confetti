CONTEXT


At the time that this poem is set, Ireland was deep in conflict; between the Nationalists and the Unionists.
The Unionists wanted to be closer with England, and the United Kingdom, and the Nationalists didn't want anything to do with England or the Untited Kingdom.
This conflict was deeply founded in religion. Unionists were typically protestant, and Nationalists were typically catholic.
Unionists typically resided in Northern Ireland, and Nationalists typically resided in the Republic of Ireland (the southern bit).

Ciaran Carson, born in 1948, is a poet and novelist from Northern Ireland. Born into an Irish-speaking family, he went on to become Professor of English at Queen's University Belfast.
He combines a life-long passion for traditional music with a deep interest in politics.

Joel Allison
